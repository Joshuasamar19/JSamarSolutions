Multidisciplinary clash detection for the UKCC Center, coordinating electrical, mechanical, and structural models to catch conflicts before they reached the field. Resolutions were reviewed directly with the design team through Autodesk Construction Cloud.

Challenge

A multidisciplinary build required catching conflicts between electrical, mechanical, and structural systems before they reached the field.

Approach

Result

🔧 Behind the process

Data Vs Duct Work

Data conduit clash detection checks for conflicts between data conduits and ductwork. It helps ensure proper routing and clearance before installation, avoiding site rework, delays.

Clash Issues

Mark Ups

Using client markups, I update and model the required changes in Revit. This ensures the 3D model follows the latest design comments and project requirements.

Navis Export

Navisworks export converts the Revit model into a coordination file for clash detection and model review. It allows project teams to combine disciplines, identify conflicts, and resolve issues before construction.

🔧 Behind the process

Electrical Room

Electrical room plans in Revit show the layout of electrical equipment, panels, conduits, cable trays, and required access clearances. They help coordinate the room for safe installation, operation, and maintenance.

Conduit Schedules

Revit conduit schedules organize conduit information such as size, type, length, and quantity. They help with material takeoff, installation planning, and project coordination.

Rack Schedule-PreFab

Revit rack schedules list rack type, size, location, and quantity, including the cut list and bill of materials (BOM). They support accurate fabrication, material takeoff, and installation planning.

Rack Installation

Rack installation involves setting out, assembling, and securing service-support racks for conduits, cable trays, pipes, or other MEP systems. It ensures services are properly supported, aligned, and ready for installation.
